Cake Storage Guide

Posted on March 10, 2026 | By Bakers Fresh

Proper cake storage is essential to maintain the freshness, moisture, and flavor of your cakes. Whether the cake is homemade or purchased from a bakery, storing it correctly can prevent it from drying out or spoiling.

Different types of cakes require different storage methods depending on their ingredients and frosting. Cakes without cream fillings can usually be stored at room temperature, while cakes containing dairy products should be kept in the refrigerator.

Room Temperature Storage

Butter cakes and sponge cakes can be stored at room temperature in an airtight container for up to two days.

Refrigeration

Cakes with whipped cream, custard, or fruit fillings should always be stored in the refrigerator.

Fondant Cake Storage

Fondant cakes should be kept in a cool, dry place and stored inside a cake box to prevent condensation.

Freezing Cakes

Cake layers can be frozen for up to three months if wrapped properly with plastic wrap and foil.
